# Script Director — AI Short-drama

## Goal

Write a shootable, speakable episodic screenplay from the approved episode plan and drama bible.

Read `references/ai-short-drama/adaptation.md` before writing.

## Timing contract

- Use the project duration as a hard budget, not an aspiration.
- Reserve time for reaction, action, transitions, and visual comprehension; dialogue cannot occupy 100% of runtime.
- Read dialogue aloud mentally at realistic Chinese speech speed. Short-form drama often needs fewer words, not faster delivery.
- The first meaningful conflict or information gap should appear within three seconds.
- The final beat must land before the cut; do not place essential dialogue after the duration budget.

## Scene construction

Each scene needs:

- a stable scene ID and numbered order;
- an executable slugline and duration;
- a clear objective, obstacle, turn, and exit state;
- characters already defined in the bible;
- actions that can be shown, not only narrated;
- dialogue line IDs for storyboard and voice references;
- source references for adapted claims or beats.

Dialogue must reveal desire, conflict, concealment, or change. Remove greetings, repeated exposition, and lines that explain what the image already shows.

## Continuity rules

- Never change a character’s established motivation merely to force a cliffhanger.
- Track what each character knows at every scene.
- Track location, time, wardrobe, props, injuries, and entrances/exits.
- Set up every later reveal and avoid impossible information transfer.

## Review

Check episode duration, hook strength, escalation, dialogue naturalness, source fidelity, and cliffhanger variety. Save one schema-valid `screenplay`, open the `screenplay` UI view, and stop for approval.
