# Director Storyboard Reference

## Shot purpose

Every shot must answer: what changes for the viewer during this shot? Valid answers include information, emotion, spatial understanding, threat, relationship, or rhythm. If nothing changes, merge or remove it.

## Coverage ladder

Use only what the scene needs:

- establishing shot for geography or a meaningful reveal;
- medium/two-shot for relationship and blocking;
- close-up for a decision, discovery, or withheld reaction;
- insert for story-critical object state;
- point-of-view when subjectivity changes meaning;
- transition shot when time/place change cannot be understood from audio alone.

## Prompt construction

Write prompts in this order:

1. approved character/location references;
2. shot size and composition;
3. subject placement and screen direction;
4. one dominant action;
5. expression and emotional intensity;
6. lighting/time/weather state;
7. lens/camera motion only when meaningful;
8. continuity constraints and negatives.

Avoid prose such as “cinematic masterpiece” unless translated into visible decisions.

## Motion feasibility

Prefer a chain of simple, controllable actions over one prompt containing dialogue, complex hand contact, crowd motion, camera orbit, costume change, and environmental destruction. Split shots when model reliability or continuity would otherwise collapse.

## Cut continuity

- Preserve left/right movement and eyelines.
- Change shot size enough to avoid jump cuts.
- Match action across cuts.
- Use reaction shots after the cause is legible.
- Protect subtitle-safe space in vertical compositions.
